How it works

Customer statuses

New — A customer who is still early in their journey with your store. They haven't purchased enough times yet for Keeply to know their personal rhythm. New customers are the most important segment to act on early — the gap between a first and second purchase often determines whether someone becomes loyal or disappears for good.

Active — A customer who is buying within their normal rhythm. No action needed — they're healthy.

At Risk — A customer whose rhythm has gone quiet. They haven't left yet, but the window to bring them back is closing. This is the most actionable segment — the sooner you reach out, the better the chance of recovery.

Lost — A customer who has been silent for a long time beyond their personal rhythm. Recovery is still possible, but statistically unlikely. Worth a long-shot win-back attempt.

Thresholds are calibrated for typical e-commerce patterns and can be adjusted in Settings.

New customer engagement

Because new customers haven't established their own rhythm yet, Keeply uses your store's typical repeat-purchase pattern to gauge how they're doing.

Warming up — Still within the normal return window for your store. No action needed yet.

Needs a nudge — They've gone past your store's typical return window. A well-timed message — a thank-you, a product tip, a gentle reminder — can make the difference between a one-time buyer and a loyal customer.

Slipping away — They've been silent for a while beyond your store's typical window. A long shot, but still worth a win-back attempt before they're gone for good.

Key metrics

ADBO — Average Days Between Orders. Each customer has their own purchase rhythm. ADBO captures that rhythm individually — not as a store-wide average. Someone who buys coffee every week and someone who buys furniture once a year can both be perfectly healthy customers. Keeply needs enough order history to compute a reliable rhythm.

Revenue at Risk. The revenue you stand to lose if an At Risk customer doesn't come back. Calculated from their recent order history using a method that resists outliers — so one unusually large purchase doesn't overstate the real risk. The headline number on your dashboard is the total across all At Risk customers.

Days overdue. How many days a customer has gone beyond their personal rhythm. The higher this number relative to their normal pattern, the harder the recovery tends to be.

Store ADBO. The typical repeat-purchase rhythm across your returning customers as a whole. Used as a reference point for new customers who haven't established their own pattern yet. A coffee shop and a furniture shop will have very different store ADBOs — Keeply adapts automatically.

Product Groups

An order belongs to a group if any of its line items carries a matching product tag. A customer appears in every group where they have qualifying orders — with group-specific rhythm and revenue metrics calculated separately for each.

The General group includes all orders and cannot be deleted. It's the default view and works well for stores that sell one type of product.

Custom groups are useful when your store spans categories with very different natural purchase rhythms. Without groups, those patterns blend together and the signals become less precise. With groups, each category gets its own rhythm and its own churn indicators.

You can create and manage groups in the Groups page.

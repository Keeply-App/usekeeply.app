Keeply

Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 25, 2026

Keeply ("we", "us", "our") is a Shopify embedded app that predicts customer churn based on individual purchase rhythm. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights as a merchant.

1. Data We Collect

When you install Keeply, we copy the following data from your Shopify store into our own database. This local copy powers our analysis — we do not query Shopify in real time when you open your dashboard.

Your customers' data:

  • First name, last name, email address, account creation date
  • Order history: order ID, date, total price, currency, financial status, cancellation date
  • Product metadata from orders: product title, type, and vendor

Your store data:

  • Shop domain, currency, timezone, store name, installation date

Your account data (from the OAuth install flow):

  • Shopify user ID, name, and email address of the staff member who installed the app

We do not collect payment card information, addresses, phone numbers, passwords, or data beyond what is listed above.

2. How We Use Your Data

We use collected data exclusively to provide the Keeply service:

  • Compute each customer's Average Days Between Orders (ADBO) — their individual purchase rhythm
  • Classify customers as New, Active, At Risk, or Lost based on their rhythm and your store's median ADBO
  • Calculate Revenue at Risk for your dashboard
  • Display customer segments and details inside your Shopify Admin
  • Send you a weekly email digest summarizing retention health, segment shifts, and top at-risk customers (only if you opt in)

We do not sell your data, share it for advertising, or use it for any purpose other than delivering Keeply.

3. Data Storage

All data is stored in a PostgreSQL database hosted on Fly.io (US East — Virginia). Access is restricted to the Keeply application only. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest.

4. Weekly Digest Email

If you enable the weekly digest, Keeply sends a summary email to the address you configure in Settings (or the store owner email by default). This email is delivered via Resend (resend.com) and includes:

  • Your store's retention health score and segment counts
  • Week-over-week status transitions
  • Top at-risk customers (name, email, revenue at risk, days since last order)

Customer names and emails are transmitted to Resend solely for rendering and delivering this email. You can disable the weekly digest at any time from Keeply's Settings page.

5. Data Retention & Deletion

We retain your data for as long as Keeply is installed on your store.

When you uninstall:

  • App sessions are cleared immediately upon receiving the app/uninstalled event
  • All customer, order, and store data is permanently and irreversibly deleted within 48 hours upon receiving Shopify's shop/redact webhook

You may also contact us at support@usekeeply.app to request early deletion of your data.

6. Customer Data Requests (GDPR / CCPA)

If one of your customers submits a data access or deletion request via Shopify, Shopify forwards it to Keeply automatically:

  • Deletion requests (customers/redact): the customer's personal data (name, email, account details) is deleted immediately upon receiving the request from Shopify. Order records are retained without customer attribution for merchant accounting purposes.
  • Data access requests (customers/data_request): we log the request and deliver the export within 30 days. Contact support@usekeeply.app if you have not received a response.

7. Error Tracking

We use Sentry (sentry.io) to capture application errors and performance data. Error reports may include the shop domain, staff session context, and internal operation metadata. We do not intentionally send end-customer personal data to Sentry.

8. Access Logging

Every read and write of customer or order data is logged with a timestamp, shop identifier, and operation type. These logs are retained for 30 days and are used solely for auditing and incident response.

We may update this policy as the service evolves. The effective date at the top reflects the latest revision. Material changes will be communicated via the Shopify Partner email on file.
